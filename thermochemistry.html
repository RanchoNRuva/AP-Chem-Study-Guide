Unit 6: Thermochemistry

Section 1: Fundamental Concepts

Temperature and Kinetic Energy

Temperature

  • Measure of average kinetic energy of particles
  • Kelvin = °C + 273.15
  • Absolute zero = 0 K = -273.15°C

Kinetic Energy and Temperature Relationship

  • Higher temperature = faster particle motion
  • More collisions at higher temperatures
  • Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution shows spread of molecular speeds
  • Broader distribution at higher temperatures

Systems and Surroundings

Definitions

  • System: Part being studied
  • Surroundings: Everything else
  • Universe: System + surroundings

Types of Systems

  • Open: Exchange matter and energy
  • Closed: Exchange energy only
  • Isolated: No exchange of matter or energy

Section 2: Energy Transfer and Heat

Forms of Energy Transfer

1. Heat (q)

  • Energy transfer due to temperature difference
  • Flows from hot to cold
  • Measured in joules (J)

2. Work (w)

  • Force × distance
  • PΔV work in gases
  • Measured in joules (J)

3. Radiation

  • Electromagnetic energy transfer
  • No medium required
  • Example: Solar energy

Specific Heat and Heat Capacity

Specific Heat (c)

  • Energy needed to raise 1g by 1°C
  • q = mcΔT
  • Units: J/g°C or J/g·K

Heat Capacity (C)

  • Energy needed to raise entire object by 1°C
  • C = mc
  • Units: J/°C or J/K

Molar Heat Capacity

  • Energy to raise 1 mole by 1°C
  • Units: J/mol·K

Section 3: Calorimetry

Calorimeter Types and Use

Coffee Cup Calorimeter

  • Constant pressure
  • Measures qsolution = -qreaction
  • Uses q = mcΔT

Bomb Calorimeter

  • Constant volume
  • Used for combustion reactions
  • More precise measurements

Calculations

Heat Exchange

  • qsystem + qsurroundings = 0
  • q = mcΔT for physical changes
  • q = ΔH for chemical reactions

Calorimeter Constants

  • Ccal = heat capacity of calorimeter
  • qcal = CcalΔT

Section 4: Chemical Energy and Bonds

Bond Energy

Definition

  • Energy required to break bond
  • Energy released when bond forms
  • Measured in kJ/mol

Bond Length and Strength

  • Shorter bonds = stronger bonds
  • Multiple bonds stronger than single bonds
  • Related to electron overlap

Potential Energy Diagrams

Features

  • Shows energy vs. reaction progress
  • Indicates ΔH of reaction
  • Shows activation energy

Interpretation

  • Uphill = endothermic
  • Downhill = exothermic
  • Peak = transition state

Section 5: Enthalpy

Enthalpy Changes

Definition

  • ΔH = heat at constant pressure
  • State function
  • Independent of path

Standard Enthalpy

  • ΔH° at 1 atm, 25°C
  • Reference state for elements

Types of Enthalpy Changes

Formation (ΔHf°)

  • Making compound from elements
  • Elements in standard state
  • ΔHf° = 0 for elements

Combustion (ΔHc°)

  • Complete reaction with O₂
  • Usually exothermic

Neutralization

  • Acid-base reactions
  • Usually exothermic

Hess's Law

Principle

  • ΔH is path independent
  • Can add reactions and ΔH values
  • Reverse reaction = opposite sign

Applications

  • Calculate unknown ΔH
  • Break complex reactions into steps
  • Use standard enthalpies
Key Concepts for AP Exam:
  • Understand energy transfer in chemical processes
  • Master calorimetry calculations
  • Know how to use Hess's Law
  • Understand relationship between bonds and energy
  • Be able to interpret potential energy diagrams
