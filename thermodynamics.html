Unit 7: Thermodynamics

Section 1: Fundamental Concepts

Systems and Surroundings

System

  • Part of universe being studied
  • Area where reaction/process occurs
  • Can be open, closed, or isolated

Surroundings

  • Everything outside the system
  • Where energy/heat can be transferred to/from
  • Rest of universe besides system

Types of Systems

  • Open: Exchange matter and energy
  • Closed: Exchange only energy
  • Isolated: No exchange of matter or energy

Section 2: Laws of Thermodynamics

First Law

  • Energy cannot be created or destroyed
  • ΔE = q + w
  • Internal energy change = heat + work
  • Conservation of energy

Second Law

  • Entropy of universe always increases
  • ΔSuniv = ΔSsys + ΔSsurr > 0 for spontaneous processes
  • Heat flows from hot to cold spontaneously
  • Defines direction of processes

Third Law

  • Entropy of perfect crystal at 0 K = 0
  • Provides reference point for entropy
  • Perfect order at absolute zero

Section 3: Entropy

Microscopic Definition

Microstates

  • Possible arrangements of particles
  • More microstates = higher entropy
  • S = k ln(W)
  • W = number of microstates

Factors Increasing Entropy

  • Increasing temperature
  • Phase changes to less ordered states
  • Increasing volume
  • Increasing number of particles
  • Breaking bonds/increasing disorder

Entropy Changes

System Entropy (ΔSsys)

  • Change in system disorder
  • Calculated from standard entropies
  • ΔS°rxn = ΣnS°(products) - ΣnS°(reactants)

Surroundings Entropy (ΔSsurr)

  • Related to heat exchange
  • ΔSsurr = -ΔH/T
  • Temperature dependent

Section 4: Spontaneity and Free Energy

Gibbs Free Energy

Definition

  • G = H - TS
  • ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
  • Combines enthalpy and entropy effects

Spontaneity Criteria

  • ΔG < 0: Spontaneous
  • ΔG = 0: Equilibrium
  • ΔG > 0: Non-spontaneous

Temperature Effects

Enthalpy-Entropy Interplay

  • Low T: ΔH dominates
  • High T: TΔS dominates
  • Transition temperature where ΔG = 0

Predicting Spontaneity

ΔH ΔS Spontaneous?
- + Always
+ - Never
- - At low T
+ + At high T

Section 5: Equilibrium and Free Energy

Relationship to Equilibrium

Standard Free Energy

  • ΔG° related to equilibrium constant
  • ΔG° = -RT ln(K)
  • K = equilibrium constant

Reaction Progress

  • ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln(Q)
  • Q = reaction quotient
  • ΔG decreases toward equilibrium

Important Relationships

Free Energy and K

  • ΔG° < 0: K> 1
  • ΔG° = 0: K = 1
  • ΔG° > 0: K < 1

Temperature Dependence

  • K changes with temperature
  • Related through van't Hoff equation
  • ln(K₂/K₁) = -(ΔH°/R)(1/T₂ - 1/T₁)
Key Concepts for AP Exam:
  • Understand entropy and its microscopic basis
  • Master free energy calculations and relationships
  • Know how to predict spontaneity
  • Understand connection between ΔG° and K
  • Be able to analyze temperature effects on processes
